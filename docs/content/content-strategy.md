## Content Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** Share real work, real numbers, real results. Client projects, internal tools, process screenshots.
2. **Lead with data.** Every claim backed by a number. "We reduced their admin time by 90%" > "We improved efficiency."
3. **Builder's lens.** Don't just comment on trends — evaluate them. "Could my client use this tomorrow?"
4. **Opinions welcome.** Have a take. The audience follows Jesus for perspective, not neutrality.
5. **Generous.** Share tools, links, frameworks, resources freely. Give value before asking for anything.
6. **No fluff.** Every post earns its space. If it doesn't teach, inspire, or prove something — cut it.

## Newsletter Voice Reference (El Tintero)

### The Reader
Business owner. Has used ChatGPT for basics. Curious about AI but overwhelmed by the pace. Busy — reads on phone, scans quickly. Wants practical value, not intellectual curiosity alone.

### Voice Rules
- Sound like a knowledgeable friend giving advice over coffee
- Direct and opinionated, not arrogant
- Enthusiastic but grounded
- Occasionally witty, never cute
- Lead with the takeaway, not the setup
- One idea per paragraph max
- Bold for key points, bullets over paragraphs

### Forbidden Jargon
- "LLM" → "AI model" or "AI assistant"
- "multimodal" → "works with text, images, and more"
- "vector database" → "AI's memory system"
- "RAG" → "AI that can search your documents"
- "prompt engineering" → "asking the AI the right way"
- If you must use a technical term, explain it immediately in parentheses.

### Opinion Rules
- Tool reviews: Always share whether you'd use it
- News: Explain why it matters (or doesn't) for a business owner
- Implementation: Share what worked for you personally
- Be neutral on competitive races (OpenAI vs. Anthropic vs. Google) and anything you haven't tried

### The Weekly "So What"
Every issue ends with a synthesis: What changed this week? What's noise vs. signal? What should they do (if anything)?
