# Content Guidelines

## General Guidelines

- Use a conversational professional tone that speaks directly to the reader using "you."
- Maintain 9th-grade reading level and A2 English level for clarity.
- Write content that is unique, plagiarism-free, and factually correct.
- Keep sentences clear, short, and easy to understand - avoid jargon unless explained.
- Follow the exact outline structure without adding or removing headings.
- Ensure no fluff, no filler - everything must add value.
- Use a we/you perspective to make the content more human-centered and engaging.
- Keep paragraphs short and readable, with strong flow between them.
- Use bullet points sparingly and only when they improve clarity (not thin points).
- Always reflect the expertise of a no-code developer, automation expert, founder, and AI specialist.
- Maintain strong topical depth, covering concepts thoroughly but simply.
- Ensure the content satisfies user intent and answers what the reader is truly looking for.
- Avoid repetitive statements or repeating the same idea in different words.
- Avoid promotional tone - keep the content helpful, practical, and educational.
- Never use bold text inside content unless the project explicitly requires it.
- Always maintain consistent formatting, clean flow, and proper grammar.
- Include examples or simple explanations wherever helpful.
- Avoid over-claiming or making unverifiable promises.
- Ensure every section transitions smoothly into the next for strong readability.

## Structure Guidelines

- Follow the exact outline hierarchy: H1 -> H2 -> H3 -> (bullet points).
- Never change, remove, or add headings unless I instruct you.
- Each H2 section should begin with a strong introductory paragraph that sets context.
- After the intro paragraph, include bullet points only when they add clarity (not thin points).
- Bullet points must be long (2 lines or 50 words) to strengthen SEO signals and topical completeness.
- Keep paragraphs short - ideally 2-4 lines each for readability.
- Avoid overly long sections; break into H3s when needed for clarity.
- Use clear transitions between sections so the article flows naturally.
- Maintain logical progression: basic -> intermediate -> advanced -> conclusion.
- If comparisons exist, structure them as separate H3 sections, not repeating content.
- Place FAQs at the end if the outline contains them.
- Ensure the conclusion summarizes key takeaways without repeating entire sections.
- Keep formatting clean: no excessive bolding, no decorative text, no unnecessary indentation.
- Wherever helpful, include simple examples to support explanations.
- Maintain consistent structure across all sections for a professional, polished article.
- Do not use tables unless I ask; rely on text and bullets instead.

## SEO & Keywords

- Use the target keyword naturally throughout the article.
- Keyword frequency: aim for 50-70 uses depending on article length.
- Add semantic keywords and related terms naturally (LSI keywords).
- Never keyword-stuff; readability comes first.
- Every section should satisfy a specific part of user intent.
- Cover all entities, subtopics, and angles expected by Google for full topical depth.
- Write with the goal of ranking in the top search results.
- Use clear, simple phrasing while keeping content information-rich.
- Maintain natural keyword placement in:
  - Heading variations
  - Section intros
  - Bullet points
  - FAQ answers
  - Conclusion
- Keep internal logic strong so Google understands topic structure clearly.

## FAQ Structure Rules

- Place FAQs at the end of the article, after the main conclusion.
- Use H3 for each FAQ question.
- Each FAQ answer must be at least 50 words.
- Avoid repeating content from the main article.
- Use simple, clear, A2-level explanations.
- Add relevant keywords naturally to strengthen SEO and topical authority.
- Keep answers helpful, direct, and practical for real user questions.

## Intro Section

- The introduction must be 130-140 words to create a balanced, engaging opening that sets context without overwhelming the reader.
- Mention the target keyword naturally within the first 1-2 lines so Google understands relevance early while keeping the tone smooth and natural.
- Start with a hook, encouraging statement, insight, or a useful statistic that grabs attention and makes the reader want to continue.
- Keep the writing conversational and professional, helping the reader feel guided, supported, and interested from the first paragraph onward.
- Break the intro into short 2-4 line paragraphs for readability, especially on mobile.
- Use any one of frameworks below, depending on the target keyword or target topic:
  - PAS - Problem, Agitate, Solution
  - AIDA - Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
  - Before, After, Bridge (BAB)
  - The Credibility Opener
  - "Myth Busting" Frame
  - "Stats & Shock" Lead
  - Question Hook Framework

## Conclusion

- Summarize the main value points of the article clearly and simply.
- Avoid repeating full explanations from earlier sections.
- Keep it short - 3 to 5 lines or 130 words maximum.
- Provide a final helpful takeaway aligned with user intent.
- Do not add new topics or heavy details in the conclusion.
