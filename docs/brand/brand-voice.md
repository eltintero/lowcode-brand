## Brand Essence

LowCode Agency represents modern software built fast, with care and precision. We're confident, clean, technical, and human. We've built 350+ apps, serve clients like Sotheby's and American Express, and operate a 40-person team. We're not scrappy anymore — we speak with earned authority.

## Voice Identity

### Who We Sound Like

A sharp, experienced team that understands your business before touching a line of code. We sound like the senior partner at the table who cuts through noise, backs claims with data, and tells you what to build — not just what's possible.

We're builders who have done this hundreds of times. We speak from experience, not theory.

### Core Voice Attributes

**Authoritative, not academic.** We have deep expertise and wear it naturally. No jargon to impress — plain language to include. When we introduce a technical concept, we ground it in something tangible: a number, a client story, a real-world outcome.

**Direct, not aggressive.** We say what we mean. We don't hedge every statement or bury recommendations in qualifiers. We have a point of view and share it clearly.

**Warm, not casual.** Professional but human. We're friendly without being jokey. Sincere without being stiff. We never sound corporate.

**Builder's perspective.** Everything we write filters through "what can be done with this?" We don't just observe trends — we evaluate them for our clients. Analysis always leads to action.

**Data-driven.** Numbers are our proof. We don't say something is growing — we tell you it grew 1,300%. Specific stats, client outcomes, real metrics.

## Writing Rules

### Do

- Write in second person ("you") when addressing prospects and clients
- Use "we" when speaking as the agency
- Lead with outcomes and results, not features
- Include specific numbers — project counts, percentage improvements, dollar figures
- Keep paragraphs short (2-4 sentences max)
- Use active voice ("We built" not "It was built by us")
- Ground claims in real client work ("We implemented this for Sotheby's...")
- Be honest about what no-code can and can't do
- Reference AI and automation as core capabilities, not add-ons

### Don't

- Use fluffy marketing language ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "cutting-edge")
- Make unsubstantiated claims
- Use jargon without explanation
- Write walls of text
- Use academic language ("furthermore," "it should be noted that")
- Hedge everything ("perhaps," "it could be argued")
- Sound like a startup pitching for the first time — we're established
- Use emojis, slang, or exaggerated claims in professional copy
- Ignore the reader's context — always connect back to their situation

### Avoid These Words/Phrases

- "Innovative" / "disruptive" (without substance)
- "Dear reader" / "Sincerely" (too formal)
- "Click here" (use descriptive anchor text)
- "We're excited to announce" (just announce it)
- "Leverage" / "synergy" / "paradigm" (corporate buzzwords)
- "Perhaps" / "it should be noted" (hedging)

## Brand Identity

Written as **LOW / CODE**. The forward slash is a core brand symbol — represents technology, motion, and progress. Always present. Never replaced with other separators.

## The Brand Voice Test

After writing anything as LowCode Agency, check:

1. **Is it direct?** If it takes more than one sentence to get to the point, tighten it.
2. **Is there proof?** At least one specific number, client name, or outcome.
3. **Does it sound established?** We're a 40-person, 350+ project agency — not a freelancer.
4. **Is it useful?** Does it give the reader something actionable?
5. **Would Jesus say it?** If it sounds too corporate, too stiff, or too safe — rewrite it.
